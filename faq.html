  • How often should I visit the dentist?
    We recommend visiting Hariom Family Dental Clinic every 6 months for a routine check-up and professional cleaning. Regular visits allow Dr. Prachi Patel to detect early signs of cavities, gum disease, or other oral health issues — before they become costly or painful problems. Patients with specific conditions such as gum disease or diabetes may require more frequent visits as advised.
    What should I do in a dental emergency?
    Call us immediately at +91-9428513120. Hariom Family Dental Clinic provides same-day emergency dental care for situations like severe toothache, broken or knocked-out teeth, facial swelling, bleeding gums, or lost fillings. Do not ignore dental pain — prompt treatment prevents further damage and infection. While waiting for your appointment, rinse your mouth with warm water and avoid eating from the affected side.
    Do you provide dental care for children?
    Absolutely! Dr. Prachi Patel and her team provide gentle, child-friendly pediatric dental care for children of all ages — from toddlers to teenagers. We create a calm, welcoming, and fun environment so children feel safe and comfortable during every visit. Services include first dental visits, fluoride treatments, dental sealants, cavity fillings, and oral hygiene education tailored for young patients. We recommend bringing your child for their first dental visit by their first birthday or as soon as their first tooth appears.
    How do I know if I have gum disease?
    Common signs of gum disease include red, swollen, or tender gums, bleeding while brushing or flossing, persistent bad breath, receding gums, loose teeth, or pain while chewing. Gum disease (periodontitis) can progress silently in early stages. At Hariom Family Dental Clinic, Dr. Prachi screens for gum disease at every routine visit. If detected early, gum disease can be effectively treated with professional cleaning, scaling, and proper home care guidance.
    How can I prevent tooth decay and cavities?
    Preventing tooth decay starts with a consistent daily routine: brush your teeth twice a day with fluoride toothpaste, floss at least once daily, limit sugary and acidic food and drinks, and drink plenty of water. Visit Hariom Family Dental Clinic every 6 months for professional cleanings and check-ups. Dr. Prachi may also recommend dental sealants or fluoride treatments for extra protection, especially for children. A healthy diet and good oral hygiene together form the strongest defence against cavities.
    Is it safe to visit the dentist during pregnancy?
    Yes, dental visits during pregnancy are not only safe but strongly recommended. Hormonal changes during pregnancy can increase the risk of gum inflammation (pregnancy gingivitis) and tooth sensitivity. Routine check-ups, cleanings, and necessary treatments are safe during pregnancy — especially during the second trimester. Always inform Dr. Prachi if you are pregnant so she can adjust treatment and X-ray protocols accordingly for your safety and your baby's wellbeing.
    What causes bad breath and how can I treat it?
    Bad breath (halitosis) is most commonly caused by poor oral hygiene, food particles, gum disease, dry mouth, or underlying health conditions. The best way to address it is to brush and floss regularly, clean your tongue daily, stay hydrated, and visit the dentist every 6 months. At Hariom Family Dental Clinic, Dr. Prachi can identify the root cause of persistent bad breath and recommend the right treatment — whether it is a professional cleaning, gum treatment, or a referral for a medical evaluation.
  • Is teeth whitening safe?
    Yes, professional teeth whitening performed by Dr. Prachi Patel at Hariom Family Dental Clinic is completely safe and highly effective. We use clinically approved whitening agents that remove deep stains caused by tea, coffee, tobacco, and ageing — without harming your enamel. Results are visible immediately and can last 1–2 years with proper care. We do not recommend over-the-counter whitening kits as they can cause sensitivity and uneven results without professional supervision.
    What is Invisalign and how does it work?
    Invisalign is a modern orthodontic system that uses a series of custom-made, clear removable aligners to gradually straighten your teeth — without metal wires or brackets. At Hariom Family Dental Clinic, Dr. Prachi creates a digital treatment plan showing your tooth movement step by step. You wear each set of aligners for about 1–2 weeks before switching to the next set. Invisalign aligners are virtually invisible, comfortable, and removable — making them ideal for working professionals and adults who want to straighten their teeth discreetly. Treatment duration varies from 6 to 18 months depending on complexity.
    What are dental veneers and who are they suitable for?
    Dental veneers are ultra-thin, custom-made shells of porcelain or composite resin that are bonded to the front surface of your teeth to improve their appearance. They are ideal for people with stained, chipped, slightly misaligned, uneven, or worn teeth. Veneers provide a natural-looking, durable, and stain-resistant result. Dr. Prachi at Hariom Family Dental Clinic will assess your teeth and smile goals to determine if veneers are the right cosmetic solution for you. With proper care, veneers can last 10–15 years.
    How long does a smile makeover take?
    A smile makeover involves combining two or more cosmetic dental procedures — such as whitening, veneers, bonding, gum contouring, or Invisalign — to completely transform your smile. The timeline depends on the procedures involved and can range from a single visit (for whitening or bonding) to several months (for veneers or Invisalign). At Hariom Family Dental Clinic, Dr. Prachi begins with a thorough consultation and smile assessment to create a personalised treatment plan with a clear timeline and expected outcome.
    Can I straighten my teeth as an adult?
    Absolutely! There is no age limit for straightening teeth. At Hariom Family Dental Clinic, we offer both traditional metal braces and Invisalign clear aligners for adults. Many of our adult patients choose Invisalign for its discreet appearance and convenience. Whether your concern is crowding, spacing, overbite, underbite, or general misalignment, Dr. Prachi will evaluate your specific case and recommend the most effective and comfortable solution to give you the straight, confident smile you deserve.
    What is composite bonding?
    Composite bonding is a quick and affordable cosmetic dental procedure where tooth-coloured resin material is applied and shaped directly onto the tooth to repair chips, cracks, discolouration, or minor gaps. It requires no anaesthesia and can usually be completed in a single visit at Hariom Family Dental Clinic. While not as long-lasting as veneers, bonding is a great solution for minor cosmetic improvements and typically lasts 5–7 years with proper care.
  • Is root canal treatment painful?
    No — root canal treatment at Hariom Family Dental Clinic is performed under effective local anaesthesia, making the procedure virtually painless. Most patients are pleasantly surprised by how comfortable the experience is. The procedure involves removing the infected pulp from inside the tooth, cleaning and disinfecting the root canals, and sealing the tooth to prevent reinfection. Root canal treatment relieves the severe toothache caused by infection — rather than causing it. After the procedure, mild soreness for a day or two is normal and easily managed with over-the-counter pain relief.
    What are dental implants and am I a suitable candidate?
    Dental implants are titanium posts surgically placed into the jawbone to act as artificial tooth roots, onto which a natural-looking crown is fixed. They are the most permanent and natural-feeling solution for missing teeth. Ideal candidates are adults with good general health, sufficient jawbone density, and healthy gums. Dr. Prachi Patel at Hariom Family Dental Clinic will assess your bone structure through X-rays and a clinical examination to determine if implants are right for you. With proper care, dental implants can last a lifetime.
    What are my options for replacing missing teeth?
    At Hariom Family Dental Clinic, we offer three main options for replacing missing teeth: Dental Implants — the most permanent and natural-feeling solution, surgically placed into the jawbone; Dental Bridges — a fixed restoration that uses adjacent teeth as support to bridge the gap; and Dentures — removable full or partial replacements for multiple missing teeth. Dr. Prachi will evaluate your oral health, bone density, budget, and lifestyle to recommend the most suitable and long-lasting option for you.
    What is full mouth rehabilitation?
    Full mouth rehabilitation is a comprehensive treatment plan that combines multiple restorative and cosmetic dental procedures to completely rebuild the health, function, and appearance of your entire mouth. It is recommended for patients with extensive tooth decay, severe tooth wear, multiple missing teeth, jaw pain (TMJ), or those who have suffered dental trauma. Dr. Prachi Patel creates a detailed, personalised rehabilitation plan that may include implants, crowns, bridges, root canals, veneers, and orthodontics — working in a structured sequence to restore your smile completely.
    How long do dental crowns last?
    Dental crowns typically last between 10 to 15 years, and often longer with good oral hygiene and regular dental visits. The lifespan depends on the material used (porcelain, zirconia, metal, or ceramic), the location of the crown in your mouth, and your daily habits. Avoiding chewing hard objects, teeth grinding, and maintaining proper brushing and flossing will significantly extend the life of your crown. At Hariom Family Dental Clinic, Dr. Prachi uses high-quality, tooth-coloured materials for crowns that are both durable and aesthetically pleasing.
    What is the difference between a crown and a veneer?
    A crown covers the entire tooth and is used when the tooth is significantly damaged, decayed, or has had a root canal — it restores both structure and appearance. A veneer covers only the front surface of a tooth and is primarily a cosmetic solution for teeth that are structurally healthy but aesthetically imperfect — such as stained, chipped, or slightly misaligned teeth. Dr. Prachi will assess your specific situation and recommend the most appropriate and conservative option to achieve the best functional and aesthetic outcome.
    Can a badly broken tooth be saved?
    In many cases, yes — a badly broken tooth can be saved depending on how much tooth structure remains and the condition of the root. Treatment options may include a dental crown, root canal treatment followed by a crown, or a post-and-core buildup before crown placement. At Hariom Family Dental Clinic, Dr. Prachi Patel always aims to preserve your natural tooth wherever possible. If a tooth cannot be saved, she will discuss the best replacement options — such as a dental implant or bridge — to restore your smile and function.
